Grandma Yetta's Meatballs

  • Back to Homepage
  • Previous Recipe

Intro: A cherished family recipe passed down through generations, these kosher meatballs are hearty, flavorful, and perfect for any gathering.

“Yetta’s meatballs were the highlight of every holiday meal. We still make them just the way she did.”

Tips: If the mixture feels too wet, add extra matzo meal gradually. Use a medium flame and rotate meatballs evenly for a perfect cook.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs 90% lean kosher hamburger
  • 1 tsp salt and pepper
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup matzo meal
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • Canola or olive oil

Instructions

  1. Grate onion into a bowl with 2 eggs and whip.
  2. Slowly add water, salt, pepper, and smashed garlic.
  3. In a separate bowl, fold the egg mixture into the meat and mix thoroughly with a large fork.
  4. Let sit for 10 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle in matzo meal and mix until meat is firm enough to form 1½ inch meatballs.
  6. If too soggy, add 2 more tablespoons of matzo meal.
  7. Place meatballs in a pan with canola or olive oil.
  8. Cook on medium flame, turning to brown all sides.
  9. Drain on paper towels and serve, store, or freeze.
Prep Time Servings
20 minutes Serves 4–6
